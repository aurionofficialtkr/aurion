About AURION 2K26

AURION 2K26 is a Hardware Project Expo organized by the Departments of EEE and ECE, TKR College of Engineering and Technology, in association with IEEE.

This is not only a presentation event. Teams move through a structured 4-stage pipeline: idea screening, panel shortlisting, prototype completion, and final live demonstration. The process emphasizes practical execution, measurable impact, technical depth, and real engineering constraints. As part of the event's industry-connect objective, winner teams will receive priority internship consideration through collaborator organizations, subject to partner requirements and final selection.

Mandatory Deliverable

A functional hardware prototype with on-spot demonstration and technical explanation.

Evaluation Model

Weighted rubric covering originality, implementation quality, impact, demo clarity, and scalability.

Technical Expectation

Projects should show engineering design choices, component-level reasoning, and feasibility under constraints.

Outcome Focus

Teams receive jury feedback, collaborator visibility, and structured guidance toward industry-readiness.

Evaluation Criteria

Jury assessment follows a weighted scoring sheet to keep evaluation transparent and consistent across teams.

  • Innovation and originality: 25%
  • Technical implementation quality: 30%
  • Problem relevance and impact: 20%
  • Presentation and demonstration: 15%
  • Feasibility and scalability: 10%

Awards and Opportunities

Winning and top-performing teams will receive formal recognition during the valedictory session.

Top teams will be acknowledged for innovation, technical execution, and practical impact.

Winner teams will also be considered for internship opportunities through collaborator organizations, subject to role availability and partner selection criteria.

Rules

Eligibility and Guidelines

  • Team size: 1 to 3 members per project.
  • Department-specific composition rule: EEE teams must submit 100% hardware projects.
  • ECE teams must submit 100% hardware-focused projects.
  • Round 1 starts with PPT-based idea screening for interested students.
  • Project ideas must be original and innovative. Copied projects are not permitted.
  • Only shortlisted teams from PPT round will proceed to final prototype completion.
  • Each shortlisted team must present a fully completed project on 17 March 2026.
  • All participants must carry valid college ID cards on event day.
  • Final evaluation decisions by jury and organizing committee will be considered final.

FAQ

Quick Answers

Who can participate?

Students from EEE, ECE and other engineering branches can register with original hardware-focused ideas.

What team size is allowed?

You can register as solo, 2-member, or 3-member team.

What file format should we upload?

Upload a single proposal file in PPT/PPTX/PDF format (up to 10 MB).

What if our problem statement is not in the list?

Select "Not in list" and provide your custom hardware problem statement.
